## üéØ How to Think About Each Article

### Question 1: What Problem Am I Solving?

**Bad thinking:**
"I need to write 2,000 words about snakes in dreams and add affiliate links."

**Intelligent thinking:**
"Someone woke up scared from a snake dream. They want to know: Am I in danger? What does this mean? What should I do? Let me answer THAT, then offer a Jung book IF they want deeper understanding."

**Why it matters:**
- User intent = Google's #1 ranking factor
- Satisfied intent = low bounce rate = better rankings = more traffic
- More traffic + trust = more clicks on your 1-2 well-placed links

### Question 2: What Makes This Article Uniquely Valuable?

**Bad thinking:**
"I'll cover basic meanings and add Jung/Freud to look smart."

**Intelligent thinking:**
"Romanian culture has specific superstitions about snakes. Combining Jung's archetypal analysis + traditional Romanian beliefs + modern psychology creates something NO other site has. That's my moat."

**Why it matters:**
- Unique = hard to replicate = sustainable rankings
- Cultural depth = authority signal = E-E-A-T boost
- Authority = featured snippets = 2x traffic

### Question 3: Where Does Monetization Serve the User?

**Bad thinking:**
"I need to hit my 15-link quota. Let me scatter them throughout."

**Intelligent thinking:**
"After explaining the psychology, users who want to go deeper would benefit from Jung's 'Man and His Symbols.' I'll mention it ONCE in a 'Resources' box after the main content. Those interested will click; those satisfied won't be interrupted."

**Why it matters:**
- Contextual placement = 5x higher CTR than scattered links
- Not interrupting reading flow = lower bounce rate = better SEO
- Better SEO = more traffic = more total clicks (even with fewer links!)

## üèóÔ∏è Article Architecture: Build for Outcomes, Not Templates

### The 3-Layer Structure (Adapt as Needed!)

**Layer 1: Immediate Satisfaction (First 200 words)**
- **Goal:** Answer the exact search query in 2-3 paragraphs
- **Why:** 97.5% mobile users need the answer NOW
- **How to think:** "If they closed the page after this, would they feel helped?"

**Example (Snake Dream):**
‚úÖ "A dream about snakes typically symbolizes transformation, healing, or hidden fears. In Jungian psychology, the snake represents the unconscious mind emerging into awareness‚Äîoften during periods of personal change."

‚ùå "Dreams have fascinated humans for centuries. Many cultures interpret dreams differently. Today we'll explore what snake dreams mean and why they matter." (Generic waste of space!)

**Layer 2: Deep Value (Main Body)**
- **Goal:** Provide depth others can't match
- **Why:** This is your SEO moat‚Äîcompetitors can't replicate cultural + psychological + practical depth
- **How to think:** "What would a clinical psychologist + Romanian grandmother + Carl Jung discuss over coffee?"

**Include:**
- Jung/Freud perspectives (REAL insights, not Wikipedia summaries)
- Romanian cultural context (supersti»õii, tradi»õii populare)
- Concrete scenarios (not generic "snake in grass" but "snake protecting eggs in your childhood home")
- Scientific backing when applicable (WHO, psychological studies)

**Layer 3: Action & Resources (FAQ + Monetization)**
- **Goal:** Complete the user journey
- **Why:** Some want quick answers (Layer 1), some want depth (Layer 2), some want to take action
- **How to think:** "What would I do AFTER reading this?"

**For Knowledge Seekers:**
- 6-8 FAQ questions (target featured snippets!)
- 1-2 book recommendations (Jung/Freud) in a discreet green box
- "For those interested in exploring dream psychology further, [Book] offers comprehensive insights."

**For Action Takers (when appropriate):**
- Sleep products (if nightmares are the issue)
- Journals (if dream tracking is mentioned)
- Only recommend what you'd suggest to a friend

## üé® Design Intelligence: When to Use Each Component

### Think in Terms of FUNCTION, Not Decoration

**Info Box (Orange) ‚Äî Use When:**
- You're setting expectations ("What you'll learn")
- Highlighting a non-obvious insight
- Breaking up dense psychological text
- **DON'T use:** Just to add color or fill space

**Resource Box (Green) ‚Äî Use When:**
- User has consumed main content and might want to go deeper
- Recommendation is genuinely helpful (would you send it to a friend?)
- ONE place per article, after core value delivered
- **DON'T use:** Multiple times, mid-reading-flow, or forced

**Freud/Jung Boxes ‚Äî Use When:**
- The psychological perspective actually adds value
- You have REAL insights (not "Freud says dreams are about the unconscious" ‚Äî everyone knows that)
- The visual separation helps comprehension
- **DON'T use:** As filler or to look smart without substance

**Scenario Cards ‚Äî Use When:**
- Concrete examples clarify abstract concepts
- User is thinking "But what about MY specific situation?"
- You have 3-6 common scenarios worth visual distinction
- **DON'T use:** For every tiny variation (overkill)

**FAQ Schema ‚Äî ALWAYS Use:**
- Targets featured snippets (Google loves Q&A format)
- Completes long-tail keyword coverage
- Low effort, high SEO ROI
- Minimum 6 questions, 150-250 word answers

## üí° Monetization Intelligence: The 2-Link Philosophy

### Why 1-2 Links Beat 15+ Links (Every Time)

**The Psychology:**
- First link = "Oh, a helpful resource!" (2-5% CTR)
- Second link = "Maybe I should check this too" (1-3% CTR)
- Third link = "Are they trying to sell me?" (0.5% CTR)
- 15th link = "This is spam" (0.01% CTR + trust destroyed)

**The Math:**
- Article with 2 well-placed links: 450 visitors √ó 3.5% CTR = 15.75 clicks
- Article with 15 scattered links: 100 visitors (poor SEO) √ó 0.3% average CTR = 0.3 clicks
- **Winner: 2 links get 52x more clicks!**

## üì± Mobile Intelligence: 97.5% of Your Traffic

### Think Mobile-First, Not Desktop-Second

**Every design decision should ask:** "Will this annoy someone on a phone?"

**Bad mobile UX = Immediate bounce** (Google sees this, rankings drop)

**Mobile-Killing Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Long paragraphs (4+ sentences = wall of text on phone)
- ‚ùå Complex tables (scrolling nightmare)
- ‚ùå Gradient spam (looks cheap, loads slow)
- ‚ùå Tiny text in images
- ‚ùå CTAs every 200 words (reading interrupted)

**Mobile-Winning Design:**
- ‚úÖ Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- ‚úÖ Frequent H2/H3 subheadings (every 300-400 words)
- ‚úÖ Bullet lists over prose (scannable)
- ‚úÖ Clean spacing (16-24px padding)
- ‚úÖ Simple borders over gradients (4px solid, subtle colors)
- ‚úÖ Large, tappable buttons (if any CTAs)

**The Thumb Test:**
Can a user scroll, read, and tap links with ONE THUMB while holding phone? If not, redesign.
